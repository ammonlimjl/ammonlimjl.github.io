How HDB valuation works in Singapore

An HDB flat's market value is driven by recent resale transactions for similar flats in the same town and block, adjusted for floor level, layout, remaining lease, renovation condition, and current buyer demand. The estimate above uses the official resale transaction records published by HDB on data.gov.sg, looking at the 25th–75th percentile of prices over the last 12 months for your town and flat type.

Why the official valuation matters when selling

When a buyer secures an Option to Purchase, HDB issues an official valuation that determines how much of the price can be financed by loan and CPF. Any amount agreed above that valuation is COV (Cash Over Valuation) and must be paid in cash. Pricing your flat correctly against valuation is the difference between a fast sale and a listing that sits for months. Most of my listings sell within 2–4 weeks.
