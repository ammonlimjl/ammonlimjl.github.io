The full comparison

Four things that actually decide it:

1Cost

BTO

Cheaper upfront. It is subsidised, so the price tag is lower.

Resale

Higher price tag. You are buying on the open market.

On price alone, BTO wins. I will not pretend otherwise.

2Waiting time

BTO

You may need to ballot a few times before you get one. That alone can be around 2 years. Then around 4 years to build. Roughly 6 years in total, and that is if everything goes smoothly.

Resale

The fastest you can move in is around 4 months.

3Choice of unit

BTO

You cannot choose your floor, facing or exact unit. You take what you are allocated.

Resale

You see the actual flat and pick the one you want.

4Grants

BTO

Comes already subsidised, that is the main help built in.

Resale

First-timer grants available for eligible buyers. The amount depends on your income and flat type, so message me and I will work out your exact figure.

So which one?

It is not about which is cheaper. It is about your situation.

If you can wait, and you ballot successfully, BTO is a great deal. If you cannot wait years, resale plus the first-timer grants changes the maths.

Most people I speak to sit somewhere in between, and that is exactly the conversation worth having.

Your situation is different from the next person's.

Tell me your timeline and I will tell you which makes sense for you.
