Buy & Sell HDB in Woodlands

Big flats, smaller prices, and the biggest infrastructure pipeline in Singapore.

Woodlands flats are among the most affordable in Singapore for their size (check the live table below), yet the town has the largest infrastructure pipeline in the country: the RTS Link to Johor Bahru, the Woodlands Regional Centre plan, the Thomson-East Coast Line already running, and the North-South Corridor on the way. Buyers get more space for their money, and owners get real reasons to expect demand to grow.

Here's the live market data and the seven things I'd weigh before buying or selling in Woodlands.

Local Knowledge

7 Things That Move Woodlands Prices

The RTS Link to JB is coming
The cross-border RTS Link at Woodlands North is targeted to open around end-2026, the north's biggest catalyst.
Woodlands Regional Centre brings jobs north
URA's plan brings around 700,000 sqm of new commercial space, and more jobs nearby.
Thomson-East Coast Line is already here
The TEL links Woodlands straight to Orchard and the city, which many buyers haven't priced in.
Woodlands Health Campus is open
The hospital and care campus is open and ramping up, adding an amenity and a workforce nearby.
More space for the money
Woodlands 4 and 5-room flats consistently price below the national median.
Causeway Point and Woods Square
An established retail core: Causeway Point plus offices and F&B at Woods Square.
North-South Corridor shortens the drive
When the corridor opens, drives to the city should shorten, something prices don't yet reflect.

Common Questions

Woodlands HDB Questions, Answered

How much does a 4-room HDB in Woodlands cost?
See the live table above, refreshed monthly from data.gov.sg. Woodlands consistently prices below the national median for its flat sizes. For your specific block, use the free valuation tool.
Is Woodlands HDB a good investment?
No honest agent promises appreciation. What I can say is that Woodlands pairs below-median prices with the RTS Link, Regional Centre, TEL and North-South Corridor, all committed. The real question is which blocks benefit most, and proximity to Woodlands North and the town centre matters.
Should I buy in Woodlands before the RTS Link opens?
Confirmed infrastructure tends to get priced in gradually, so waiting until the line opens usually means paying more. If the RTS Link matters to you, focus on blocks within a comfortable walk of Woodlands North and negotiate on today's comparables. I'll send you the block-level numbers.
How is the rental demand for Woodlands flats?
Steady and likely improving: cross-border commuters, Woodlands Health staff, and the northern industrial workforce all rent here, and the RTS Link should deepen that pool. If you're choosing between selling and leasing out, I'll run both numbers for your unit.
